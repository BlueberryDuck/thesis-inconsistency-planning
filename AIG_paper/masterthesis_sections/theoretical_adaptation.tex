\section{Theoretical Adaptation}
\label{sec:theoretical_adaptation}

This chapter presents three proposals for adapting inconsistency measures to classical planning problems. This work develops planning-native measures that operate directly on planning structures: goals, operators, and precondition-effect relationships. The approach employs multi-faceted diagnostic profiles rather than single severity scores, providing actionable information for problem repair without making assumptions about which repairs are valid.

The chapter begins with a structural taxonomy for unsolvable planning problems (Section~\ref{sec:taxonomy}), followed by motivation for planning-native measures (Section~\ref{sec:naive_encoding}) and six test scenarios (Section~\ref{sec:test_scenarios}) that serve as running examples. Section~\ref{sec:proposed_measures} presents the three measure proposals with formal definitions. Section~\ref{sec:theoretical_properties} analyzes rationality postulates and computational complexity. Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses normalization considerations.
